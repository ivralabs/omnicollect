# OmniCollect Vision

*Locked 2026-06-23*

## The Big Picture

OmniCollect is not an analytics tool. It is the data infrastructure that the entire out-of-home advertising industry has been missing for 70 years.

When OmniCollect operates at scale it becomes:

1. **The measurement standard** — independent third-party audience verification for OOH globally. Like Nielsen for TV.
2. **The programmatic data layer** — the real-time audience data that enables automated OOH buying across Africa and beyond.
3. **SA's mobility intelligence platform** — the most comprehensive vehicle and pedestrian dataset on the continent, valuable across retail, property, insurance, urban planning, and investment.

## The 6 Layers

### Layer 1 — Foundation
Replace ROAD survey data (last updated 2019) with real-time, site-specific audience data. Immediately valuable to every media owner in SA.

### Layer 2 — Network Effect
500+ sites across SA = unique reach measurement + audience journey mapping. The more sites, the more valuable the data. Competitors can never catch up on dataset depth.

### Layer 3 — Programmatic Play
OmniCollect becomes the data layer that enables real-time pDOOH buying. Global programmatic OOH market projected at $12B+ by 2030. OmniCollect owns the African data feed.

### Layer 4 — Verification Standard
Independent audit of media owner audience claims. Agencies mandate "OmniCollect-verified sites only." Media owners must join to compete for premium budgets. This is the network tipping point.

### Layer 5 — Retail Intelligence Crossover
The same cameras that measure OOH audiences generate footfall and mobility data valuable to retailers, property developers, municipalities, insurers, and investment funds. Pure additional margin.

### Layer 6 — AI Forecasting Engine
2–3 years of data → OmniCollect predicts audience composition before a campaign runs. Seasonal patterns, event spikes, route shifts. Becomes an AI campaign strategist available to every media owner on the platform.

$500M–$2B within 10 years.

Not because the technology is exotic. Because OmniCollect owns the data that an entire industry runs on — and data compounds. A competitor starting today is always behind.
