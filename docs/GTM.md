## The Approach

**Who to talk to:** Sales Director or Commercial Director (not CEO, not IT)

**Opening question:** *"When an agency asks you to verify your audience figures, what do you tell them?"*

Let them describe the pain. Don't pitch until they've talked.

**The offer:**
> "We install on 3 of your sites — your choice. 90 days, completely free. No contract, no obligation. At the end you'll have real audience data. If it doesn't improve your rate card conversations, you walk away."

## Conversation Flow

**Meeting 1 — Discovery (30 mins)**
Ask, listen, don't pitch. Questions:
- "What's your biggest challenge pitching premium sites to agencies?"
- "How do you currently justify your audience figures?"
- "Have you lost campaigns to digital because of measurement concerns?"
- "Do your screens already have cameras?"

**Meeting 2 — Data Demo (45 mins)**
Show realistic SA data. Not a product demo — a data demo.
- Hourly vehicle counts for a typical Sandton site
- Vehicle class breakdown (32% SUV, 28% sedan, 18% bakkie)
- Before/after rate card — same site, estimated vs verified, 40% premium justified

**Meeting 3 — Pilot Agreement (15 mins)**
One page. No lawyers. 3 sites, 90 days, free.

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| Data privacy | All processing on-device. No video leaves camera. POPIA compliant by design. Only hashes + counts to cloud. |
| Old/low-res cameras | Works on 360p. We count and classify, we don't record. |
| IT approval needed | Small edge device, plugs into existing camera output. IT has full visibility. We can do a walkthrough. |
| Existing analytics contract | Additive. Run alongside. Switch when contract comes up. |
| What happens to data after pilot? | It's yours. We use aggregated anonymised network data only. No individual site data shared without permission. |

## Rollout Sequence

**Month 1:** Sign one media owner, 3 sites live
**Month 2:** Use their data as case study for second conversation
**Month 3:** Second media owner. Now 6 sites, two reference customers.
**Month 4:** Walk into Publicis/GroupM with two logos and real data.

Once an agency specifies OmniCollect-verified sites → inbound from media owners begins. Third, fourth, fifth conversations become pull not push.

## Camera Placement Strategy

**Priority 1 — Major arterials** (money roads)
Johannesburg: William Nicol, Grayston, Jan Smuts, N1, N3, M1
Cape Town: N2 Airport corridor, De Waal Drive, Buitenkant/Adderley

**Priority 2 — High-dwell intersections**
Traffic lights with long cycles, taxi ranks, fuel stations. 60–120 second dwell = maximum processing time.

**Priority 3 — Affluence corridors**
Sandton, Stellenbosch, Umhlanga, Constantia. Premium data for premium rates.

**Priority 4 — Commuter nodes**
Train stations, BRT stops, taxi ranks. High frequency, predictable daily patterns.

**Key insight:** 80% of LSM 8–10 commuter traffic in Johannesburg passes through ~15 key intersections. Camera those 15 and you have the premium dataset. Coverage comes over time.

## The Sequence Rule

**Do not launch OmniCollect before Deckly has traction.**

1. Deckly gets first paying customer
2. Deckly reaches 10 paying customers
3. OmniCollect quiet pilot — 1 media owner, 3 sites
4. Both grow in parallel (same customer base)
5. Bundle emerges naturally

Splitting focus across two products with zero customers = how startups die.
