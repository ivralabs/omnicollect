| Exit Target | Required ARR (at 10×) |

## What Drives the Premium Valuation

Pure SaaS businesses exit at 6–8× ARR. The data asset is what pushes the multiple to 10–15×.

**The moat:**
- Years of proprietary real-time traffic data across 20+ African markets
- No competitor can replicate this dataset overnight — it compounds every year
- OmniCollect becomes infrastructure, not just software
- First-mover position in the most underserved OOH measurement market globally

This is exactly the Ipsos model. They don't just sell software — they license measurement data as an industry currency. Agencies and brands *need* the data to plan and buy campaigns. That dependency is what commands a premium exit.

## The Single Biggest Unlock

**Getting one of the big three SA players as a customer.**

JCDecaux, Pimedia, or Provantage — a signed enterprise contract with any of them is a global calling card.

- It proves the tech works at scale
- It opens doors in Lagos, Nairobi, Dubai, Singapore
- It signals to acquirers that OmniCollect is industry infrastructure, not a startup

> Box Board proves the tech. One major SA player proves the business.

---

## Key Principle

Build the data asset from day one. Every billboard installed, every vehicle counted, every brand recognised — that's the asset appreciating in the background while the SaaS revenue grows.

The exit isn't just buying the software. It's buying the data.
